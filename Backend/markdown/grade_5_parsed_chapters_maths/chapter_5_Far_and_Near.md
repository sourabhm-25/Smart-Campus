# Multiplying and Dividing Lengths

1. We need a 1 m 80 cm cloth to make a shirt for a 10-year old child. How much cloth will be needed to make shirts for 20 such children?
   $$20 \times 1 \text{ m } 80 \text{ cm} = 20 \times 1 \text{ m and } 20 \times 80 \text{ cm}$$
   $$= 20 \text{ m } + 1600 \text{ cm}$$
   $$= 20 \text{ m } + 16 \text{ m}$$
   $$= 36 \text{ m.}$$
   > Break the quantity into m and cm and multiply. You can also convert it into cm and multiply.

# Let Us Explore

In daily life, we use other units of measuring length as well. Height is usually measured in feet and inches. Look at your ruler to see if inches are also marked on it. Find out how many cm and mm equal 1 inch.

*centimetres*

[An illustration of a yellow ruler showing a centimeter scale. The numbers 1, 2, and 3 are marked. Below the ruler, a blue bracket labeled "1 inch" spans from the 0 mark to the 2.5 cm mark and halfway to the next millimeter mark.]

1 inch = 2 cm 5 mm
1 foot = 12 inches or 30 cm.
