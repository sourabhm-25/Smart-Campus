## Alphabet Cutout

Prem and Manu want to paste ‘Happy Birthday’ cutouts on a wall for Lali’s birthday. While preparing cutouts of letters, they observe that some letters can be cut out in an easy way.

They remember that they learnt about reflection symmetry and lines of symmetry in Grade 4. They used their knowledge of lines of symmetry to make the cutouts. The letter A has a vertical line of symmetry. So, to cut out the letter ‘A’—

1. Fold a paper in half.
2. Draw half of the letter A along the fold.
3. Cut along the outline.
4. Open the paper to see the full letter A.

# Let Us Make a Windmill Firki

Lali makes firkis for her friends. Follow the steps given below to make your own *firki*.

1. Take a square paper. (A yellow square paper is shown)
2. Fold the paper in half diagonally to make two triangles. (The paper is folded into a triangle)
3. Open and fold it the other way to make two more triangles. (The paper is opened showing one diagonal crease, then another image shows it with two diagonal creases forming four triangles)
4. Open it again. You will see an ‘X’ shape on the paper. (The paper is shown flat with an 'X' crease)
5. Use scissors to cut along the four lines of the ‘X’. Stop cutting about halfway to the centre. (Scissors are shown cutting along one of the crease lines towards the center)
6. Take one corner of each triangle and fold it gently towards the centre of the paper. Do not press it flat. (One corner of the paper is folded to the center)
7. Fold every other corner towards the centre. (Four alternating corners are folded to the center, forming the firki shape)
8. Push a pin through the folded corners and the centre of the paper. (A pin is shown being pushed through the center of the folded corners)
9. Push the pin through a stick or straw. (The firki is shown attached to a wooden stick)

> Make sure the pin is not too tight.
>
> Check if your windmill spins when the wind is blowing.
>
> (An illustration shows a girl happily holding her spinning firki)
